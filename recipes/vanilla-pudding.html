Vanilla Pudding

Ingredients

  • 2 Large egg yolks
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 2 Tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 Tbsp softened butter
  • 2 tsp vanilla

Steps

  1. Get Thick

    Put your eggs in a heatproof bowl, beat them and set them aside. In a 2 quart saucepan, mix your sugar, cornstarch and salt. Pour in the milk gradually to avoid lumps and put over medium heat. Stir the milk mixture constantly until it gets thick and starts boiling.

  2. Temper the Yolks

    Here's the deal, we need the richness and thickness that our yolks can supply, but if we direct deposit those little guys into our hot milk, they'll cook right up. We'll end up with milk-poached egg yolk instead of rich, thick pud. So we need to be smart. Take half of the hot milk, and gradually stir it into the egg yolks. Once that is combined we can add it back into the main saucepan. Let it boil and sit together for 1 minute, and then remove it from heat. Finish it off with the butter and vanilla.

  3. Chill

    If you like warm pudding good for you, but I have deep psychological hang ups that prevent me from indulging in such things. Divvy up your goop into dessert dishes and cover them with plastic to prevent a skin from forming. They should chill in the fridge for at least an hour. Pudding you don't immediatly consume in your gluttony can be stored covered in the fridge for as long as you're brave enough to eat it.
